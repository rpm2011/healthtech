Problem
Definition

Design a solution that empowers individuals to direct themselves to the most appropriate site of care, guaranteeing patients get the best care for the lowest cost, while minimizing costs for the insurer.

Next

Defining
Our Problem

Original Prompt

A patient-centric mobile application that minimizes inappropriate ER usage

Technological advances are revolutionizing the healthcare industry, but the focus is on the hardware technology, rather than the patient engagement experience. There is an enormous opportunity for digital interactions to empower individuals to be more responsible and more effective at managing their own care. Human-centered design can ameliorate challenges such as retrieving healthcare information, locating appropriate care options, communicating directly with physicians, and scheduling appointments.

Minimizing inappropriate emergency room usage reduces inefficiencies in cost and time within the healthcare system. This provides an incredible business incentive to encourage patients to engage in preventive healthcare measures, rather than reactionary treatments. With this in mind, research, design, and prototype a patient-centric mobile application that targets adults ages 18 to 54, provides information about healthcare providers, and maximizes the volume of appropriate care facility use.

Next

Change over Time

The project prompt suggested we explore fixes for immediate medical issues, such as providing information through a mobile app on distance to care, displaying average treatment times, or building communication channels with physicians.

Over the course of several months, we researched the decision making criteria, pain points, challenges, and successes of urgent medical visits. We explored ways in which we could provide a more holistic healthcare experience and direct individuals to an appropriate place of care when needed.

Next

Research Findings

01

It is almost impossible to persuade someone on what actions to take at the time a medical incident occurs, due to their preconceived notions and emotional state.

02

To successfully change behaviors, it is necessary to engage individuals over a prolonged period of time, and re-educate and remove bad habits while teaching new good habits.
